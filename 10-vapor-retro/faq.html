Frequently Asked

Tuition, schedule, licensing, GI Bill, job placement — everything you've wondered about the American Barber Institute, answered.

How long does the Master Barber program take?

The 500-Hour Master Barber program runs about 17 weeks (≈4 months) full-time. Weekend students typically complete in 27 weeks.

Do I need prior experience to enroll?

No prior experience is required. We train you from the ground up, starting with the fundamentals and progressing to advanced cutting, fading, beard work, and shaving.

Is ABI licensed and accredited?

Yes. ABI is licensed by the New York State Department of Education through the Bureau of Proprietary School Supervision (BPSS).

What's the tuition and how do I pay?

Tuition ranges from $4,600 to $5,600 depending on schedule. Every plan starts with a small down payment and continues with low weekly payments — full breakdown on the Programs page.

Do you accept GI Bill® or VA benefits?

Yes. We accept Post-9/11 GI Bill®, VA benefits, and ACCES-VR. Our admissions advisors will walk you through the paperwork.

When is the next class start?

Our next start date is Monday, July 6, 2026. We accept new students on a rolling basis — contact admissions for the next available cohort.

Where are your campuses?

Manhattan: 48 West 39th Street, New York, NY 10018. Bronx: 121 Westchester Square, Bronx, NY 10461.

What hours can I train?

Three flexible tracks: Morning (Mon–Fri 8AM–2PM), Afternoon (Mon–Fri 2PM–8PM), and Weekend (Sat–Sun 9AM–7PM).

Do you help with job placement?

Yes. Job placement services are included. Our network includes Levels, Diamond Fadez, Untouchable Cutz, Otis & Finn, and the NYC Barber Shop Museum.

What skills will I learn?

Modern fades, haircutting, beard work, straight-razor shaving, styling, color, sanitation, business basics, and NY State Board Exam preparation.

Can I get a haircut at the school?

Yes — our student clinic offers haircuts from $3 (haircuts), giving our trainees real chair time while you get a great cut.

Do you offer financial assistance?

Yes. We work with veterans (VA, GI Bill®), ACCES-VR participants, and offer in-house weekly payment plans for every program.

How big are the classes?

We keep instructor-to-student ratios low to ensure every student gets real hands-on time on the floor.

What happens after I graduate?

You take the NY State Board Exam, earn your license, and start working. Our placement team helps you land your first chair.

Do you offer Spanish-language admissions?

Yes — Spanish-speaking advisors are available. Call (212) 290-0278 for Spanish admissions at the Manhattan campus.
