FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Tuition, schedule, licensing, GI Bill®, ACCES-VR, job placement, tools — everything you've wondered about ABI, answered.

How long does the Master Barber program take?

The 500-Hour Master Barber program can be completed in as little as 4 months full-time (Morning or Afternoon, Mon–Fri). The Weekend track typically completes in 6–7 months. The 50-Hour Refresher is a 2-week intensive. The 3-Hour Contagious Diseases course is a single afternoon.

Do I need prior experience to enroll?

No prior barbering experience is required. We train you from the fundamentals — sanitation, posture, blade angle — and progress to advanced fading, beard work, straight-razor shaving, color, and chair-side business skills.

When do new classes start?

New classes begin the first Monday of every month. The next cohort starts Monday, July 6, 2026. Apply, gather your documents, and be ready for the next start date — there's almost never a long wait.

What are the entrance requirements?

A Social Security card, a High School Diploma / GED (or pass the ATB exam at ABI), proof of address, a valid photo ID, being at least 17, and a $500 down payment (which includes a $100 registration fee).

How much does the program cost?

The 500-Hour Master Barber program is $4,600 on the Afternoon and Weekend tracks; the Morning track is $5,600. The 50-Hour Refresher is $1,500. The 3-Hour Contagious Diseases course is call-for-pricing. All programs include flexible weekly payment plans after the down payment.

Do you offer payment plans?

Yes. Every program includes a weekly payment plan after your down payment. Examples: Morning is $500 down + 17 weekly payments of $300. Afternoon is $500 down + 16 weekly payments of $250 + $100 final. Weekend is $550 down + 27 weekly payments of $150.

What license will I earn?

On completing the 500-Hour Master Barber program you'll be fully prepared for the New York State Board of Barbering exam — both written and practical — and your Master Barber License.

Do you offer job placement?

Yes. On graduation you'll meet with our Job Placement Office for 1-on-1 career counseling, resume and portfolio review, mock interviews, and direct referrals to our partner shops including Levels, Diamond Fadez, Otis & Finn, Untouchable Cutz, Expo Gentlemen, and the NYC Barber Shop Museum.

Do you accept GI Bill® or VA benefits?

Yes. ABI accepts the Post-9/11 GI Bill® (Ch. 33), VR&E (Ch. 31), Montgomery GI Bill®, and DEA (Ch. 35) education benefits. We certify your enrollment with the VA on your behalf.

What is ACCES-VR?

ACCES-VR is the NY State Adult Career and Continuing Education Services — Vocational Rehabilitation program. It supports individuals with documented disabilities and may cover tuition, tools, and supplies. Talk to your local ACCES-VR office, and we'll handle the school-side paperwork.

Is ABI licensed by the state?

Yes. American Barber Institute is licensed by the New York State Department of Education through the Bureau of Proprietary School Supervision (BPSS), and we've served New York for over 30 years.

Can I get a haircut at ABI?

Yes — the public is welcome for affordable student haircuts (typically $3). It's a great way to support trainees while getting a clean, supervised cut. Booking: (856) 316-1551.

Do I need to buy my own tools?

Yes. Students are responsible for purchasing their own barbering tools, books, and supplies. ABI provides a detailed tools list at registration, with the option to buy directly from us or from approved third-party suppliers.

Do you offer Spanish-speaking admissions?

Yes. Spanish-speaking admissions advisors are available. Call (212) 290-0278 to speak with Spanish admissions at the Manhattan campus.

How big are the classes?

We keep instructor-to-student ratios low so every student gets real hands-on time on the floor. You're not learning by lecture — you're learning by chair time, in a working clinic, with an instructor inches away when you need them.
